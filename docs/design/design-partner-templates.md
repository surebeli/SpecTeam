# SpecTeam Design Partner Templates

This document provides copy-paste templates that turn the design partner playbook into day-to-day operating material.

Edit the placeholders, keep the structure, and use the same formats across all partner conversations.

## How To Use This Document

1. use the outreach templates to start conversations
2. use the discovery and guided-session templates to run calls consistently
3. use the note and checkpoint templates to record evidence in the same shape every time
4. use the paid-pilot template only after real workflow pull appears

## Template 1 - Cold Outreach Message

Use this for founders or small teams you do not know directly.

```text
Subject: Reducing spec drift across your team and AI tools

Hi {name},

You may already have this problem: your PRD says one thing, your architecture doc says another, and your AI tools generate different paths.

I'm working on SpecTeam, a workflow that helps product and engineering teams detect spec divergence, resolve decisions faster, and keep humans and AI tools aligned.

If this is happening in your team, I'd like to compare notes and see whether the current workflow matches your reality. Not a sales pitch, just a 20 to 30 minute problem-fit conversation.

Would {time option A} or {time option B} work?

Best,
{your name}
```

## Template 2 - Warm Intro Message

Use this when you have a shared connection.

```text
Hi {name},

{mutual contact} suggested I reach out. I'm working on SpecTeam, a workflow for teams that keep hitting spec drift between PRDs, architecture docs, and AI-generated proposals.

We're seeing early interest from small product-engineering teams that use multiple AI tools and need a faster way to detect divergence and close decisions.

If that sounds familiar, I'd like to run a short working conversation and compare your current process with the workflow.

Open to a 20 minute chat next week?
```

## Template 3 - Inbound Qualification Reply

Use this when someone asks for a demo or more detail.

```text
Thanks for reaching out.

Before we schedule time, can you share one recent example where your PRD, architecture doc, or AI-generated proposals contradicted each other?

The best first session is when we use a real example from your current workflow. If the problem is active, we can either start with a short discovery call or go straight into a guided session.
```

## Template 4 - Discovery Call Agenda

Use this for the first conversation when the problem still needs diagnosis.

```markdown
# Discovery Call - {team}

## Goal
- Understand whether spec divergence and decision alignment are painful enough to justify repeat usage.

## Timebox
- 5 min: context
- 10 min: current workflow
- 10 min: pain intensity
- 5 min: product fit and next step

## Questions
1. What kinds of decisions create the most confusion for your team?
2. Where do contradictions usually appear first: PRDs, architecture docs, or AI-generated proposals?
3. What happens today when two specs conflict?
4. Who decides what is final?
5. How do you know the final decision made it back into the source documents?
6. How expensive is this problem in time, rework, or wrong decisions?
7. If a tool could detect divergence and help close decisions faster, where would it fit in your workflow?
8. What would need to be true for your team to use that repeatedly?

## Exit Criteria
- Clear yes or no on whether the pain is active
- One real example to use in a guided session
- Next action agreed
```

## Template 5 - Guided First-Use Agenda

Use this once the team has a real example.

```markdown
# Guided First-Use Session - {team}

## Goal
- Reach first value using one real divergence case.

## Inputs Needed
- Two conflicting specs, proposals, or documents
- One current decision worth discussing
- One person who can confirm whether the result is useful

## Success Path
1. Prepare the conflicting inputs
2. Run divergence detection
3. Pick one disagreement worth resolving
4. Review and align on a decision
5. Confirm where the updated source of truth now lives

## Observation Checklist
- Did the team understand the product quickly?
- Was setup friction acceptable?
- Did participants understand who proposes, approves, and finalizes?
- Did they trust the recorded outcome?
- Did they name another real decision where they would use it?

## Exit Criteria
- One decision resolved or clearly scoped
- Team can explain the workflow back in its own words
- Follow-up real use scheduled within one week
```

## Template 7 - Follow-Up After First Session

Send within 24 hours.

```text
Thanks again for the session.

The most important thing I want to confirm is whether this workflow feels useful enough to bring into a real decision next.

From the session, I noted:
- What worked: {summary}
- What felt confusing: {summary}
- Next real decision candidate: {summary}

Would you be open to trying this on {next decision} within the next week? If yes, I can help make the follow-up session lightweight.
```

## Template 9 - Lightweight Paid Pilot Proposal

Use only when the team has already shown real workflow pull.

```text

The next thing worth testing is a lightweight paid pilot focused on {visibility | approvals | templates | decision history}.

The goal of the pilot would be:
1. shorten decision time further
2. make the workflow easier to repeat across the team
3. validate whether this layer is valuable enough to fund

If useful, I can draft a small pilot scope with:
- success criteria
- timeline
- support level
- pilot price
```
